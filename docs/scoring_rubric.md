# CSA-Lite Scoring Rubric

## Overview

Each dimension is scored 0, 1, or 2 based on public-record evidence.
Null is used when evidence is genuinely insufficient — not to indicate
a "middle" position.

**Important:** Scores are structured-coding outputs from public records.
They are NOT legal risk classifications, compliance determinations, or
harm predictions.

---

## 1. Decision Criticality

*How consequential are the decisions the system influences or makes?*

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 0 | Advisory or low-consequence; no material impact on access, rights, or welfare |
| 1 | Consequential but reversible; affects access or resources but with normal recourse |
| 2 | Life-altering; affects liberty, welfare, migration status, health access, education access, or livelihood |

---

## 2. Autonomy

*How automated is the system's role in decision-making?*

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 0 | Weak advisory; humans make decisions independently of the system |
| 1 | Decision support that materially influences human decisions |
| 2 | Automated / de-facto-determinative / practically difficult for humans to override |

---

## 3. Vulnerability

*How vulnerable is the affected population?*

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 0 | General adult population with typical institutional access and resources |
| 1 | Asymmetric institutional context: students, workers, borrowers, applicants, tenants, claimants |
| 2 | Heightened vulnerability: minors, patients, asylum seekers, migrants, detainees, welfare recipients, suspects |

---

## 4. Oversight

*Is there meaningful human oversight with real authority to override the system?*

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 0 | Documented meaningful review with clear override authority |
| 1 | Nominal, partial, unclear, or inconsistent oversight |
| 2 | No meaningful oversight; rubber-stamp review; review occurs only after harm is discovered |

---

## 5. Recourse

*Can affected individuals meaningfully challenge decisions?*

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 0 | Clear, accessible, timely appeal pathway documented and practically available |
| 1 | Unclear, partial, slow, costly, or inconsistently applied recourse |
| 2 | No meaningful recourse; practically unavailable due to cost, complexity, or opacity |

---

## 6. Scale

*How many people are affected?*

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 0 | Small pilot or limited internal use |
| 1 | Institutional, municipal, regional, or sectoral deployment |
| 2 | National, platform-scale, high-volume, or population-scale deployment |

---

## 7. Opacity

*How transparent is the system's operation?*

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 0 | Public documentation sufficient to understand methodology and decision logic |
| 1 | Partial opacity; key details are missing but some information is available |
| 2 | Proprietary, secret, or unexplained; meaningfully unchallengeable due to opacity |

---

## 8. Monitoring

*Is there documented post-deployment monitoring?*

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 0 | Documented audit, monitoring, or evaluation with published results |
| 1 | Unclear or informal monitoring; partially documented |
| 2 | No documented monitoring; problems discovered externally or through incident reporting |

---

## Computing the Context Severity Index (CSI)

CSI (neutral) = sum of known scores + 1 for each null score

This is a transparent structured-coding aggregate. It is NOT a legal risk class.

Severity bands:
- **Low:** 0–3
- **Moderate:** 4–7
- **High:** 8–11
- **Severe:** 12–16
